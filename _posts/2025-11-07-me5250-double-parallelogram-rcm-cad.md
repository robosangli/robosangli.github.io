For the first project in my Robot Mechanics and Control (ME 5250) course at Northeastern University, I explored design and analysis of a double parallelogram remote center of motion mechanism.

I performed a survey of the existing literature to explore the mechanism’s ability to rotate around a fixed point without having a physical revolute joint. I also performed a mobility analysis to show why the mechanism has 2 DOF while still having a total joint count of 9. I also explored the model's kinematics in terms of the 2 angles (2 DOF). The image below shows the simplified kinematics model used for the analysis.

[simple kinematics model photo]

This project introduced me to the complex mechanics of remote-center-of-motion mechanisms, particularly double-parallelogram mechanisms.
